The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) launched on 18 February 2016 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi is an insurance service for farmers for their yields.[1] It was formulated in line with One Nation-One Scheme theme by replacing earlier two schemes National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS) and Modified National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (MNAIS) by incorporating their best features and removing their inherent drawbacks (shortcomings). It aims to reduce the premium burden on farmers and ensure early settlement of crop assurance claim for the full insured sum.

<h2>Objective of the Scheme: </h2>     

* Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) aims at supporting sustainable production in agriculture sector by way of providing financial support to farmers suffering crop loss/damage arising out of unforeseen events Stabilizing the income of farmers to ensure their continuance in farming.<br>
* Encouraging farmers to adopt innovative and modern agricultural practices Ensuring flow of credit to the agriculture sector which will contribute to food security, crop diversification and enhancing growth and competitiveness of agriculture sector besides protecting farmers from production risks.

<h2>Coverage of the farmers:</h2> 

All the farmers growing notified crops in a notified area during the season who have insurable interest in the crop are eligible.

<b>Compulsory coverage : </b>The enrollment under the scheme, subject to possession of insurable interest on the cultivation of the notified crop in the notified area, shall be compulsory for following categories of farmers:<br>

1 Farmers in the notified area who possess a Crop Loan account/KCC account (called as Loanee Farmers) to whom credit limit is sanctioned/renewed for the notified crop during the crop season. and<br>
2. Such other farmers whom the Government may decide to include from time to time.

<b>Voluntary coverage : </b>Voluntary coverage may be obtained by all farmers not covered above, including Crop KCC/Crop Loan Account holders whose credit limit is not renewed.

<h2>Coverage of Crops:</h2>

1. Oil seeds<br>
2. Food crop<br>
3. Annual Commercial / Annual Horticultural crops.<br>
In addition for perennial crops, pilots for coverage can be taken for those perennial horticultural crops for which standard methodology for yield estimation is available.

<h2>Apply for Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana</h2>

Farmers who have taken loans from banks or co-operative societies can enrol through the same bank. And, farmers who have taken no loans can apply through banks, brokers, CSC e-Governance Services India Limited or directly through the website. To apply for online crop insurance under PMFBY, follow the given steps-

1. Visit <a href="https://pmfby.gov.in/">PMFBY Website</a>.<br>
2. Click on -> <b>Farmer Corner </b><-<br>
3. If you are already registered, you can proceed to get enrolled under the scheme. However, new farmers will have to register themselves by filling the registration form.
